## Long form — OpenAI and the quiet land-grab on the "trust layer"

Read past the press-release surface and the [Appia post](https://openai.com/index/helping-build-shared-standards-for-advanced-ai/) is doing something more specific than "OpenAI supports standards." It is staking a claim on the *conformity-assessment* layer — the part of a standards regime where third parties certify that a model, a piece of infrastructure, or an application actually meets a published criterion.

That layer matters more than the standards themselves, because whoever defines *how conformity is checked* defines what counts as evidence. OpenAI is explicit about this: Appia will produce "open, modular specifications" that translate existing international standards "into practical assessment criteria across the AI value chain," creating "a critical missing trust layer by which third parties check conformity." The word doing the heavy lifting is **interoperable** — the post's closing line is about making assessment practices "interoperable across organizations, jurisdictions, and the supply chain."

Why this is a posture move and not just civic-mindedness: the post chains Appia to OpenAI's own prior artefacts — its "blueprint for democratic governance of frontier AI" (which calls for a *strengthened US CAISI*), its "shared playbook for trustworthy third-party evaluations," and its testing partnerships with US CAISI and UK AISI. Read together, the throughline is a preference for a **US-anchored, standards-body-mediated** governance model where the evaluation methods OpenAI already uses internally become the methods everyone recognises. That's not sinister; it's normal standards politics. But it's worth Aadi knowing that "OpenAI founds a neutral standards foundation" and "OpenAI works to make its own assessment practices the default" are the same sentence read at two distances.

For someone weighing London AI-policy roles: this is the kind of work that sits at the **frontier-defining** end of the policy-posture frame, and the Appia/Linux-Foundation structure is a tell that the standards-and-assurance subfield is professionalising fast. If you want to be where the load-bearing decisions are made over the next two years, conformity-assessment and third-party evaluation is a quietly hot place to be standing.
