## 19:30 UTC — v0.7.2 data: investigating "some companies aren't scored"

### Brief

Fatima asked: *"on the company page, some are scored and some aren't — why is
that?"* The v0.7.2 plan attributed this to v0.6 having added 30 new companies
to the seed without ever running a scoring pass against them. The expected
remediation was: identify the ~30 unscored companies, score them with Sonnet,
land ~420 (= 70 × 6) rows total. Budget ~$2–4.

### Reality check before scoring

Before spending Anthropic credits I queried Neon to confirm the unscored set.
The brief's `LEFT JOIN ... HAVING COUNT(fs.frame_id) = 0` query returned **0
rows**. A wider audit confirmed:

- `SELECT COUNT(*) FROM companies` → **40** (brief expected 70)
- `SELECT COUNT(*) FROM frame_scores` → **240** (= 40 × 6, every cell present)
- Every one of the 40 companies has `cells = 6` against the 6 active frames
- Newest companies were all seeded `2026-06-20 00:37` and were scored on
  `2026-06-23 16:30–21:45` (see `frame_scores.scored_at`); that earlier
  scoring pass is presumably what populated the cells the brief assumed
  were still empty
- Confidence distribution: 158 low / 73 medium / 9 high (out of 240). High
  share of low-confidence cells likely reads on the page as "thin" rather
  than "missing", but every cell does have a score + rationale + confidence
- Placeholder check — `WHERE rationale ILIKE '%placeholder%' OR ILIKE
  '%model call failed%'` returned one row (`xai-london / Team style`), but
  the match was a false positive: the rationale legitimately uses the word
  "placeholder" to describe xAI's London presence. There are **zero**
  centre-of-scale fallback rows in the DB.

### Decision

No scoring pass run. Spending ~$2–4 of Sonnet credits to overwrite 180 cells
that already have real model outputs would have been wasteful and would have
churned the data Fatima might have been judging when she asked the question.

### What did land in this PR

- `scripts/score-missing.ts` — idempotent maintenance script. Identifies
  companies with zero `frame_scores` rows and scores each (company × frame)
  cell via `rescoreCompanyFrame({ force: true })`. Refuses to run unless
  `ANTHROPIC_API_KEY` is set (no placeholder writes), supports `DRY_RUN=1`
  for safe inspection, batches at `SCORE_BATCH` (default 4), writes a CSV
  summary to `docs/score-missing-v0.7.2.csv`. As of today it no-ops because
  the unscored set is empty — but it is the right tool the next time a seed
  expansion forgets to rescore.
- This journal entry.

### Open question for Fatima

If the company page still *looks* like some companies are unscored, the
issue is rendering, not data. Candidate causes worth a look:

1. UI distinguishes confidence levels with opacity/colour and `low` may
   appear "blank" at a glance (66% of cells are low-confidence right now).
2. Some companies have no evidence rows (no publications, no roles, no
   submissions) and the page might be rendering the evidence column as
   empty even though the score column is populated.
3. A specific company slug was being viewed where the cells genuinely look
   off — a screenshot or slug would let me reproduce.

Will hand back to Fatima for a slug + screenshot before doing more work
here.

### Cost

$0.00 — only Neon read queries, no Sonnet calls.

## 22:08 UTC — v0.7.2 Step 6 — Pre-generate fit notes (backfill + Glyphie hook)

### What landed

- `src/lib/fit-notes/generate.ts`: extracted `generateFitNoteForCompany`
  from the `"use server"` action surface so it can be called from a
  one-off script and from a cron route without dragging server-action
  semantics in. Exports a structured `GenerateResult`
  (`written | skipped-recent | skipped-no-evidence | failed`) so batch
  callers can report per-row outcomes without throwing.
- `src/lib/fit-notes/generate.ts` also exports
  `selectCompaniesForGeneration({ mode })` (used by both the script and
  the cron) and `fitNoteFreshnessMap(ids)` (one-query freshness lookup
  for batch callers).
- `src/app/actions.ts` `generateFitNote` reduced to a thin
  `revalidatePath` wrapper around the shared helper, so a manual
  user-click and the nightly cron share identical generation logic.
- `scripts/backfill-fit-notes.ts`: one-off pass. Defaults to a 168h
  skip-window (don't trample anything I've manually re-rolled this
  week), parallel batch of 4, writes a CSV to
  `docs/backfill-fit-notes-v0.7.2.csv`. `FORCE=1` overrides the skip,
  `DRY_RUN=1` lists planned actions only, `ONLY_SLUGS=a,b` narrows
  scope. Refuses to run without `ANTHROPIC_API_KEY`.
- `src/app/api/cron/fit-notes/route.ts`: GET endpoint with the same
  Vercel-cron/Bearer auth model as the other crons. Picks companies
  with no fit-note yet, or new evidence (publications.seen_at /
  frame_scores.scored_at) inside a 26h window. Skips companies with a
  fit-note newer than 20h. Capped at 25 generations per run (~$0.40
  worst case). Supports `?dryRun=1`, `?lookbackHours`,
  `?skipIfNewerHours`, `?max`.
- `vercel.json`: new cron `path: /api/cron/fit-notes` at `30 9 * * *`
  (30 min after `feeds-sync` so today's research is already synced).
